Types

Known Types:

  • Aves: When an Entity is a bird of some kind.
  • Serpentes: When an Entity is a snake of some kind.
  • Leporidae: When an Entity is a rabbit of some kind.
  • Testudine: When an Entity is a turtle of some kind.
  • Draco: When an Entity is a dragon of some kind.
  • Monstrosity: When an Entity has many unknown things about them and have something shocking, like tentacles, they are called Monstrosity.
  • Monster: When they didn't used to be the way they are now.
  • Shadow Creature: When an Entity is a creature of the shadows.
  • Mythical: When an Entity comes from myth.
  • Humanoid: When an Entity resembles a human, like body structure, but isn't one.
  • Quadrupedal: When an Entity walks on 4 legs.
  • Bipedal: When an Entity walks on 2 legs.
  • Digitigrade: When an Entity walks on their toes.
  • Plantigrade: When an Entity walks on the soles of their feet.
  • Unguligrade: When an entity walks on hooves.
  • Food: When an Entity is made of food.
  • Armor: When an Entity naturally has armor.
  • Cetacean: When an Entity resembles a marine mammal like dolphins, whales, or porpoises.
  • Arachnid: When an Entity has characteristics of spiders, such as multiple legs, spinnerets, or silk-producing abilities.
  • Arachnoid: When an Entity exhibits spider-like traits without actually being an arachnid species.
  • Feline: When an Entity has characteristics of feline species, such as cats, lions, or tigers.
  • Canine: When an Entity resembles canine species like wolves, foxes, or domestic dogs.
  • Equine: When an Entity takes the form of a horse or horse-like creature.
  • Insectoid: When an Entity possesses insect-like characteristics, such as exoskeletons or antennae.
  • Anthropomorphic: When an Entity has human-like traits but maintains a non-human appearance, akin to anthropomorphic animals.
  • Reptilian: When an Entity exhibits reptile-like features, such as scales and cold-blooded characteristics.
  • Ichthyoid: When an Entity resembles fish or aquatic species.
  • Amphibian: When an Entity displays traits of amphibious creatures like frogs, salamanders, or newts.
  • Elemental: When an Entity is associated with one of the classical elements (earth, water, fire, air) and possesses related abilities.
  • Undead: When an Entity has returned from the dead, featuring zombie-like characteristics or ghostly attributes.
  • Vertebrae: When an Entity possesses bones or bone-like structures in their body.
  • Invertebrae: When an Entity does not possess bones or bone-like structures in their body.
  • Bioluminescent: When an Entity is capable of creating their own light.
  • Octopada: When an Entity resembles an octopus of some kind.
  • Amorphous: When an Entity's appearance is able to change at will.
  • Digital: When an Entity is digital.
