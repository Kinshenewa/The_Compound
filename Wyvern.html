Wyvern

Name: Unknown
Nicknames: Silent frosty, the Struggle Snuggler
Types: Mythical, Serpentes, Draco, Vertebrae
Level: 3
Diet: Omnivore
Preferred Food: Reindeer
Handler: Jonathan Prescot
Languages: Spanish, English

Special Containment Procedures: To ensure the safety of staff and the entity, Unknown must be sedated adequately to prevent aggression but not to the extent that she falls asleep.

Notes:

  1. She rarely speaks and refrains from disclosing her actual name.

Personality: She is calm and collected, and is patient with all. At times she can lose her temper, but she never directs it to something living. Reserved and withdrawn.

Appearance: She is a greyish blue with light blue fur. She has small creamy antlers, and small blue eyes. She is 9 feet long from snout to tail, and has been seen wrapping around her Handler for comfort. She also has feathery wings, but those are rarely seen.

Warnings:

  1. She possesses remarkably sharp teeth. She refrains from harming what she calls "Light Souls" but may not hesitate to cause harm to male employees. Notably, her male handler, Jonathan, has not suffered significant harm thus far.

Abilities:

  1. She possesses a unique ability to perceive life, a concept she chooses not to elaborate on.
  2. She can breathe both ice and fire. However, the use of her breath powers leads to a noticeable change in her body temperature. Exhaling ice heats her up, while fire-breathing causes her temperature to drop. She employs this power in moderation.

Room: A medium security, elemental resistance level 3 room.

Room Number: 222

Permissions: She is allowed to go into the yard twice a week when wearing a harness to keep her in the yard. She can bring in anything she finds if she is allowed to.

History/Info: She was discovered in the Himalayan Alps, where she was revered as a deity by the local population, who offered sacrifices to appease her. While her fondness for reindeer as a source of sustenance is evident, much of her history remains shrouded in mystery. Tales recount her saving slaves, facilitating their escape from their masters. This history aligns with the presence of a population in the region who share South American heritage. The villagers have passed down stories of the great Wyvern rescuing their ancestors from enslavement, liberating them from their shackles and leading them to the village.
